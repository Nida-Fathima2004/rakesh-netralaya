Common Eye Conditions

What is Cataract?

A cataract is a clouding of the natural lens of the eye. It is the most common cause of vision loss in people over age 40 and is the principal cause of blindness in the world. It usually develops slowly and can affect one or both eyes. Modern treatment involves surgical removal.

  • Symptoms: Cloudy/blurry vision, fading of colors, poor night vision.
  • Treatment: Surgical removal and replacement with an artificial intraocular lens (IOL).
Consult a Specialist

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, the health of which is vital for good vision. This damage is often caused by an abnormally high pressure in your eye. It is one of the leading causes of blindness.

  • Symptoms: Often no symptoms initially, leading to silent vision loss. Peripheral vision loss is common.
  • Treatment: Eyedrops, oral medication, laser treatment, or surgery to lower eye pressure.
Consult a Specialist

Dry Eye Syndrome

Dry Eye Syndrome is a common condition that occurs when your tears aren't able to provide adequate lubrication for your eyes. This can be due to insufficient tears or poor quality tears. It causes discomfort and sometimes vision problems.

  • Symptoms: Stinging, burning, scratchiness, sensitivity to light, and watery eyes.
  • Treatment: Artificial tears, prescription eye drops, or minor procedures like punctal plugs.
Consult a Specialist
